WELLNESS DAYS


Join us in our upcoming Wellness Day where we prioritize your health. Because you matter.

Non-communicable diseases like Cancer, Diabetes, Chronic Respiratory Illnesses, and others that claim millions of lives have recently become more prevalent and are of worldwide concern. These illnesses that, to a certain extent, can be prevented if people recognize their health status and lead healthier lifestyles.

It is our responsibility as healthcare professionals to ensure that we promote health awareness and give Batswana the resources and encouragement they need to live better lives.Through our Wellness activations we aim to reduce the burden of NCDs and their modifiable risk factors.

Kalafi Medical Center has hosted or attended approximately over 300 wellness days since its inception in 2018. Initially, Wellness Days were birthed through the community health project “Target 10,000” which was aimed at providing free medical services to 10,000 people in Gaborone and surrounding communities.

Our Wellness Days are hosted at Main Mall in Gaborone. Follow us on our social media platforms to be informed.




Our Volunteer Program

Volunteer at Our Wellness Days

Volunteering helps you satisfy your innate desire to help others, which we all have. You can make new friendships while using or or developing skills such as sociability, communication, etc. Although volunteering at our Wellness Days does not offer references, it provides something more valuable. Experience. Volunteering with us can put experienc on your resume and provide you with the opportunity to give back to community.

To join our volunteer program. Check out the key skills and competencies we are searching for below.

Key responsibilities and tasks include:
- Conducting health screening on clients
- Distributing flyers as and when required by the Organization;
- Registering clients on the registration form and client database;
- Contact clients who have conducted health screenings;
- Health Education – Inform the public about the importance of health and encourage people to do regular health screenings;

Key skills or education:
- Nurses
- Graduates of university students in the health or public health field, or any different field however, with an interest in health.
- University Students
- Phlebotomists
